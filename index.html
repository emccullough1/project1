Helena Bonham Carter

Basic Information

Age: 52

Hometown: Islington, London, England

Biography

Helena Bonham Carter is an actress known for her versatility. Her first acting job was at the age of sixteen, in a commercial. Soon she was acting in movies, a few of her first being A Pattern of Roses (1983), A Room with a View (1985), and Lady Jane (1986). She soon moved to larger roles, like Marla Singer in the well-known movie Fight Club (1999). In the late 1990s, Carter moved from being merely and actress to a star in films such as The Theory of Flight (1998) and Mighty Aphrodite (1995).

She has made seven movies with director Tim Burton, and was nominated a fifth time for a Golden Globe and awarded Best Actress for her work in his adaptation of Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street (2007). Along with those she has received a BAFTA Award, a Screen Actors Guild Award, and many other awards and nominations.

Notable Achievements

  • Won the 2010 International Emmy Award for Best Actress
  • Was made a Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE)
  • Appointed to national Holocaust Commission in January 2014
